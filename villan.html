HERO

Little

Mr. Little, born in Omaha during the midst o’ spring, of the year 1925. Of Earl and Louise’s brood, he was the fourth of seven little ones. Son of a baptist minister and chapter president of Garvey’s Universal Negro Improvement Association. Son of the division's secretary, as well. Not even two years old was the first time Mr. Little moved homes alongside his family. Wasn’t long until it happened again, two years after his first. The Little family was evicted hardly a year later solely based on their lack of porcelain skin. They couldn’t even leave before the home went up in flames, literally. Two years, again. It was three-hundred and sixty-five days times two when Mrs. Little had a feeling that Mr. Little Senior should not leave home that day. But he went out, and his soul left earth’s graces that day. Killed in a “Streetcar Accident” so said the police.
