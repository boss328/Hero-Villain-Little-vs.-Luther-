VILLAN

Little

Not even two years old was the first time Mr. Little moved homes alongside his family. Wasn’t long until it happened again, two years after his first. The Little family was evicted hardly a year later solely based on their lack of porcelain skin. They couldn’t even leave before the home went up in flames, literally. 

Two years, again. It was three-hundred and sixty-five days times two when Mrs. Little had a feeling that Mr. Little Senior should not leave home that day. But he went out, and his soul left earth’s graces that day. Killed in a “Streetcar Accident” so said the police. 

Widowed Mrs. Louise Little would later become diagnosed as mentally-ill, and sent away to Kalamazoo State Mental Hospital for the following twenty-six years 

“The Ballot or the Bullet” 

Giving ultimatums to those who hold the unjust talking stick of power, threatening to conjure up another bloody revolution if not granted the exact demands.

Demanding equality through the lens of passionate distaste for unity between black and white Americans. 

Malcolm X: Intelligent, determined, passionate, and a visionary. Worthy adversary to the Reverend Martin Luther King Jr. 

 Is it really that surprising when your home is set ablaze when you’re so little to become a grown up that throws gas into the flame.

Similar to a phoenix, who rises from the ashes of its predecessor, Mr. Little did just that, and trail blazed through the streets preaching his extreme rhetoric. 

How do people attempt to fight prejudice with more prejudice and expect desired results? Trying to move the vast concrete mountain range of prejudice against black people, with the freshly poured concrete that is prejudice towards white people that is so easily moldable… how can prejudice be fought with prejudice successfully? It can’t, it will always come back to burn you. You reap what you sow, fighting prejudice with prejudice would just create this never ending time loop that white people started in the first place. 

It takes a person with a big ego to start this vicious cycle, it takes a humble heart and strong mind to put an end to it. 

If you aren’t a person to try and end the cycle, rather than repeat it, what makes you so righteous? Malcolm X might’ve continued to cycle, if he had the backing wall of supporters. 

I suppose it’s not really that surprising when your home is set ablaze as a young, so little child, to become a grown up that throws gas into the flame.
